# UG Formation Checklist

**Unternehmergesellschaft (haftungsbeschränkt) Gründungs-Checkliste**

---

## 1. Pre-Incorporation Steps

Complete these before scheduling your notary appointment.

- [ ] **Choose company name** — Verify name availability via the Handelsregister (commercial register) and ensure it complies with German naming regulations (contains "UG" or "Unternehmergesellschaft" and "haftungsbeschränkt")

- [ ] **Determine share capital** — Minimum €1 required; recommend €500–€1,000 for practical liquidity. Document the exact amount for the articles of association.

- [ ] **Define business purpose (Gegenstand)** — Write a clear, concise description of the company's intended business activities for inclusion in the articles of association.

- [ ] **Select shareholders and initial managing directors** — Decide who will be shareholders (Gesellschafter) and who will serve as the first managing director(s) (Geschäftsführer). Prepare full names, addresses, and dates of birth.

- [ ] **Prepare shareholders' identity documents** — Gather valid passports or ID cards for all shareholders. Non-EU shareholders may require additional documentation.

---

## 2. Notary Appointment Preparation

Ensure you have everything ready before meeting with the notary.

- [ ] **Book notary appointment** — Schedule with a German notary (Notar). Request estimated costs and confirm they handle UG incorporations.

- [ ] **Prepare articles of association draft** — Have a draft of the Gesellschaftsvertrag ready, including: company name, registered office, share capital, business purpose, shareholders and their contributions, and managing director provisions.

- [ ] **Gather all required documents** — Bring:
  - Valid ID/passport for each shareholder
  - Proof of address for shareholders and managing directors
  - Share capital contribution proof (if already deposited)
  - Marriage certificate or prenuptial agreement (relevant for spousal property regimes)

- [ ] **Confirm share capital deposit method** — Decide whether to deposit share capital before incorporation (Bareinlage, in a trustee account) or after registration (providing proof later). Coordinate with the notary on their preferred approach.

---

## 3. Post-Incorporation Tasks

Complete these steps after the notary appointment to activate your UG.

- [ ] **Deposit share capital** — Transfer the agreed share capital to the company's business account or trustee account. Obtain written confirmation of deposit (Einzahlungsbestätigung).

- [ ] **Apply for business registration (Gewerbeanmeldung)** — Register your trade at the local Gewerbeamt. Bring: notarized articles of association, ID, and Gewerbeanmeldung form.

- [ ] **Open business bank account** — Open a company bank account using the notarized documents and proof of capital deposit. Required for ongoing business operations.

- [ ] **Submit tax registration (Steuerliche Erfassung)** — Complete the Fragebogen zur steuerlichen Erfassung via ELSTER (online tax portal). Register for corporate tax (Körperschaftsteuer), trade tax (Gewerbesteuer), and VAT (Umsatzsteuer) if applicable.

- [ ] **Wait for Handelsregister entry** — The notary submits documents to the Handelsregister. Entry typically takes 1–3 weeks. You'll receive official notification once registered.

- [ ] **Set up accounting and bookkeeping** — Establish a proper bookkeeping system from day one. Engage a Steuerberater (tax advisor) if you don't handle this in-house. Maintain records for invoices, expenses, and payroll.

---

## Notes

- **Timeline:** Full incorporation typically takes 2–6 weeks from notary appointment to Handelsregister entry.
- **Costs:** Expect approximately €500–€1,500 total (notary fees, court fees, possible legal advice).
- **Obligation:** UGs must retain 25% of annual profits until share capital reaches €25,000, at which point you may convert to a GmbH.
